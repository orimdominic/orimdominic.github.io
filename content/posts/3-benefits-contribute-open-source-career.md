You've heard about open source, git, GitHub and collaboration. You're
interested. Or maybe you're wondering if this is something that you want to do.
This article hopes to detail 3 benefits that contributing to open source will
contribute to your career. In a nutshell, contributing to open source will

1. broaden your experience and increase your expertise on a topic,
2. improve your communication and your collaboration skills,
3. grow your network within programming communities

## Broaden Your Experience and Increase Your Expertise

Experience comes with the use of one's skills or knowledge. Broad experience
comes with using those skills in a wide area

How to contribute

Write articles, improve documentation While reading docs - these help put less
stress on the authors in responding to issues that your article or documentation
covers. Report bugs. Respond to issues by asking for more info or adding more
info - well-defined problems are easier to solve by maintainers, answer
questions on stackoverflow

What to contribute to

- there are lots of articles on the the technical details of PRs and stuff.
  Outside the scope of this article
- work on a project that solves a problem for you. get to a point, need help.
  ask for contribs. Usually others will have this problem too
- invest in contributing to a project that solved or has solved a problem for
  you. The thing here is that you're doing it out of gratitued
- invest in contributing to a project that you want to learn about

It's not a bad thing to be driven by what you'll benefit from doing something,
as long as it would not harm someone else. In this case, other people, including
you, benefit from your contributions to open source
